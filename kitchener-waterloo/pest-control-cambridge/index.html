Why Cambridge Homes Need Specialized Pest Control

Cambridge is a city built on rivers. The Grand River, Speed River, and Mill Creek wind through its three historic cores — Galt, Preston, and Hespeler — creating a network of green corridors that wildlife use as highways into residential neighbourhoods. Raccoons, skunks, and squirrels follow these waterways right into attics, sheds, and garages, especially during fall and winter when they seek shelter from plunging temperatures.

The city’s heritage housing stock presents its own challenges. Galt’s downtown is filled with 19th-century mill workers’ homes built from local stone and brick. These beautiful structures have settling gaps in foundations, aging mortar joints, and original wooden sills that are magnets for carpenter ants. A mature carpenter ant colony can cause structural damage equivalent to termites if left untreated — and Cambridge’s older homes are especially vulnerable.

Rodents are Cambridge’s most consistent year-round pest. The Speed River corridor provides cover and food sources that sustain large populations of Norway rats and house mice. Each fall, these rodents migrate into nearby homes through gaps as small as a dime. Our Cambridge pest control strategy starts with a thorough exclusion inspection — we find and seal every potential entry point before deploying targeted interior treatments.
