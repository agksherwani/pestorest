Why New Hamburg Properties Need Specialized Pest Control

New Hamburg is the heart of Wilmot Township, a semi-rural community where quiet residential streets transition directly into active agricultural land. Like neighbouring Elmira to the north, this field-to-structure boundary means that every harvest season brings a predictable wave of mice and rats migrating from plowed fields into homes, garages, sheds, and barns. Properties along the edges of town — and throughout Baden, Mannheim, and Wellesley — are especially vulnerable to this annual rodent invasion.

Beyond rodents, New Hamburg’s older housing stock faces a different set of challenges. Many homes in the historic core feature original wooden beams, joists, and sills that are prime targets for carpenter ants, particularly where moisture from aging roofing, plumbing, or poor drainage has softened the wood. Silverfish are another common complaint in older New Hamburg homes, thriving in the humid conditions of unfinished basements, poorly ventilated bathrooms, and attic spaces where they feed on paper, wallpaper paste, and stored fabrics.

Wildlife is a year-round concern across Wilmot Township. Raccoons, skunks, and squirrels exploit roof vents, damaged soffits, and gaps in older construction to nest in attics and crawl spaces. The rural landscape surrounding New Hamburg, Breslau, and Baden provides abundant habitat for these animals, and they readily move into residential structures when food or shelter opportunities present themselves. Our humane removal and permanent exclusion approach comes with a 2-year warranty — if wildlife returns, so do we.
