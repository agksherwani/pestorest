Our Integrated Pest Management Approach

IPM is not simply a fancy name for spraying less. It is a systematic, science-based approach to pest management that begins with understanding why pests are present in a facility and addresses the root causes rather than merely treating symptoms. Every PestoRest commercial program starts with a comprehensive facility assessment where our licensed technician inspects every area of your property, documenting current pest activity, identifying structural vulnerabilities, evaluating sanitation practices, and mapping potential entry points.

From this assessment, we develop a customized IPM plan that includes a monitoring program using glue boards, rodent monitoring stations, pheromone traps, and visual inspections at defined intervals. Monitoring allows us to detect pest activity early, before it becomes an infestation, and to track trends over time. Every monitoring device is documented on a facility map, and data from each service visit is recorded and available for health inspections and audits.

Treatment within our IPM programs is targeted and proportional. We use the least invasive effective method for each situation, prioritizing exclusion, sanitation corrections, and mechanical controls over chemical application. When chemical treatment is necessary, we use products approved for use in food-handling environments, applied in targeted locations rather than broad-spectrum application. This approach minimizes chemical exposure for staff and customers while maximizing effectiveness against the specific pests present.

Your Commercial Pest Prevention Checklist

  • Seal all gaps around exterior doors, loading docks, and utility penetrations
  • Install door sweeps and air curtains on frequently opened exterior doors
  • Maintain a clean dumpster area with lids closed and regular pickup
  • Store food products off the floor on shelving, away from walls
  • Clean drains regularly to prevent drain fly breeding
  • Address plumbing leaks and condensation promptly
  • Train staff to report pest sightings immediately using your reporting protocol
  • Keep pest control documentation accessible for health inspections
