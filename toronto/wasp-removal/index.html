Increased Wasp Activity

Types of Wasps in Toronto

Toronto is home to several wasp species, each with different nesting habits and aggression levels. Yellowjackets are the most aggressive and most commonly responsible for stings. They build concealed nests underground in old rodent burrows, inside wall voids through gaps in brick or siding, and occasionally in attic spaces. A single yellowjacket nest can contain over five thousand workers by late summer, making them extremely dangerous when disturbed. Paper wasps are less aggressive but build exposed, umbrella-shaped nests under eaves, porch ceilings, and deck railings throughout Toronto's residential areas.

Bald-faced hornets construct large, football-shaped nests in trees, shrubs, and occasionally on the sides of buildings. These nests are common in Toronto's tree-lined neighbourhoods and parks, and the hornets are highly defensive of their nests. Mud daubers are solitary wasps that build small mud tubes on walls and ceilings, primarily in garages and sheds. While they are not aggressive, their presence is unsightly and can indicate other pest activity since they hunt spiders to feed their larvae.

Preventing Wasp Nests on Your Toronto Home

Prevention is the best approach to wasp management. PestoRest recommends sealing gaps in soffits, fascia, and along rooflines each spring before queen wasps begin nest building. Repairing damaged screening on vents and covering gaps around window-mounted air conditioners reduces access points.
