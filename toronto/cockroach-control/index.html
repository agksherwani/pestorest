Warning Signs

6 Signs You Have Cockroaches in Your Toronto Home

🪫

Live Roaches at Night

Seeing cockroaches scatter when you turn on a light at night, especially in kitchens and bathrooms, indicates an active infestation.

💩

Pepper-Like Droppings

Tiny dark specks resembling ground pepper found in kitchen drawers, along countertop edges, under sinks, and behind appliances.

👃

Musty Oily Odour

A distinctive oily, musty smell in kitchens, pantries, or under sinks. Heavy infestations produce a noticeable, persistent odour.

🧹

Egg Casings

Small brown, capsule-shaped egg cases (oothecae) found in crevices, drawer joints, and behind appliances. Each contains up to 40 eggs.

🔨

Smear Marks

Dark, irregular smear marks along walls and surfaces where cockroaches travel through moisture, especially near water sources.

👁

Daytime Sightings

Seeing cockroaches during the day indicates a severe, overcrowded infestation where roaches are being pushed out of hiding spots.

Professional Cockroach Treatment in Toronto

German cockroaches are the most common and most difficult cockroach species to control in Toronto. These small, fast-breeding insects thrive in the warm, humid environments found in kitchens and bathrooms across the city's multi-unit housing. From the high-rise apartments of St. James Town and Regent Park to the condo towers along the waterfront, German cockroaches exploit shared plumbing, electrical conduits, and garbage chute systems to spread rapidly from unit to unit. Restaurants along Queen West, Dundas and Spadina in Chinatown, and the bustling food courts of Scarborough and North York face constant pressure from cockroach populations drawn to food preparation areas.

PestoRest Pest Control takes a science-based approach to cockroach elimination in Toronto. We use targeted gel baits and insect growth regulators (IGRs) that cockroaches consume and share with the colony through trophallaxis, the process of regurgitating food to nestmates. This approach is far more effective than surface sprays, which scatter cockroaches to new hiding spots and create pesticide resistance. Our technicians are trained specifically in German cockroach biology and the unique challenges of Toronto's multi-unit buildings.

Why German Cockroaches Thrive in Toronto

Toronto's housing density creates ideal conditions for German cockroach infestations. In older apartment buildings along Dundas West, in Parkdale's Victorian-era conversions, and in the sprawling rental complexes of Scarborough and North York, cockroaches travel freely between units through the building's infrastructure. A single pregnant female cockroach can produce thousands of descendants within a year. The shared garbage rooms, recycling areas, and compactor systems common in Toronto buildings provide constant food sources, while the steam pipes and hot water risers that run through every floor maintain the warm, humid conditions German cockroaches prefer.

Restaurants face equally intense pressure. Toronto's competitive dining scene spans from high-end establishments on King Street to the diverse food vendors of Kensington Market and the bakeries and noodle houses of Chinatown. In these commercial settings, cockroaches find everything they need: warmth from cooking equipment, moisture from dishwashing areas, and abundant food residue in hard-to-clean crevices behind stoves, under refrigerators, and inside wall-mounted equipment. A cockroach sighting during a Toronto Public Health inspection can result in a conditional pass on DineSafe, directly impacting customer trust and revenue.

Gel Bait and IGR Treatment Protocol

PestoRest's cockroach treatment protocol for Toronto properties centres on two proven technologies: commercial-grade gel bait and insect growth regulators. Gel bait is applied in small, precise dots at cockroach harbourage points including hinge areas of kitchen cabinets, the motor compartment of refrigerators, the undersides of countertops, behind dishwashers, and along the edges of baseboards where cockroaches travel. The bait attracts foraging cockroaches, who consume it and carry residual toxicant back to the colony. Through trophallaxis, the active ingredient spreads through the population, reaching cockroaches that never leave their hiding spots.

IGRs complement the bait by disrupting the cockroach reproductive cycle. Applied as a spray to harbourage areas, IGRs prevent nymphs from reaching reproductive maturity and cause adult females to produce inviable egg cases. This two-pronged approach attacks the current population with bait while preventing the next generation from establishing. For severe infestations in Toronto apartments and restaurants, we typically schedule two to three visits spaced two weeks apart to break the entire breeding cycle.

Cockroach infestations in rental units must be addressed by landlords at their own expense. PestoRest provides the documentation and treatment records that landlords need to demonstrate compliance.
