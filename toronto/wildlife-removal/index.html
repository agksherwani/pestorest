Warning Signs

6 Signs You Have Wildlife in Your Toronto Home

👂

Nighttime Thumping

Heavy thumping, scratching, or walking sounds in the attic or walls between dusk and dawn, typical of raccoons moving around.

💩

Droppings on Roof

Large, dark droppings on the roof, near downspouts, or in the attic. Raccoon latrines are a health hazard containing roundworm eggs.

🧀

Damaged Soffits or Vents

Torn soffit panels, bent roof vents, or damaged fascia boards where raccoons or squirrels have forced their way into the attic.

👃

Strong Urine Smell

A pungent ammonia or musky odour emanating from the attic, walls, or under a deck, indicating established animal habitation.

👁

Tracks on Roof

Muddy paw prints on downspouts, window ledges, or roofing material, showing the travel routes animals use to access your home.

🦝

Overturned Green Bins

Repeatedly toppled green bins and scattered garbage are a strong indicator of raccoon activity on your Toronto property.

Raccoon Removal in Toronto

Raccoons are by far the most common wildlife invader in Toronto homes. These intelligent, powerful animals target attics as denning sites, entering through roof vents, plumber's mats, soffit intersections, and any gap or weakness they can exploit in your roofline. Once inside, raccoons cause extensive damage: they tear apart insulation to create nests, soil attic spaces with urine and feces, and can damage electrical wiring and ductwork. Raccoon latrines in attics are a serious health concern because their feces can contain Baylisascaris procyonis, a roundworm whose eggs become airborne and can cause severe illness in humans.

In Toronto neighbourhoods like Riverdale, The Danforth, High Park, Roncesvalles, and the Annex, raccoons are a year-round presence. The Don Valley ravine system, the Humber River corridor, and High Park itself serve as wildlife highways that bring raccoons into direct contact with residential areas. During baby season from March through June, female raccoons seek out sheltered attic spaces to raise their kits, making this period especially critical for prompt action. PestoRest's technicians always check for babies before installing exclusion devices, adjusting our approach during nesting season to ensure the mother and her kits are reunited outside the home before entry points are sealed.

Squirrel Removal in Toronto

Eastern grey squirrels and red squirrels are persistent attic invaders across Toronto, particularly in neighbourhoods with large mature trees. Squirrels gnaw through plastic vent covers, roof edge flashing, and soffit panels to access attic spaces, where they build large nests of leaves and insulation. Their gnawing is especially dangerous when it targets electrical wiring, creating a genuine fire hazard. In Rosedale, Forest Hill, The Beaches, and Leaside, where century-old trees tower over rooflines, squirrel intrusions are an annual concern.

PestoRest removes squirrels using one-way exclusion doors installed at the primary entry point, then reinforces all potential secondary entry points with galvanized steel mesh. We inspect the attic for gnaw damage, particularly on wiring, and recommend repairs where necessary.

Skunk Removal in Toronto

Skunks commonly den under porches, decks, and sheds across Toronto. While they rarely enter the home itself, their digging damages landscaping, and the risk of being sprayed makes them a serious nuisance. In areas like The Junction, Bloor West Village, and Etobicoke, skunks thrive in the back gardens and older deck structures that provide easy access to sheltered denning sites. PestoRest uses one-way exclusion screening around deck and porch perimeters to evict skunks humanely and prevent re-entry.
