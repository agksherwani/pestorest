Warning Signs

6 Signs You Have Cockroaches

🌙

Live Roaches at Night

Cockroaches scatter when you turn on a light in the kitchen or bathroom at night. This is the most common first sign of an active infestation.

💩

Droppings

Small dark specks resembling ground pepper or coffee grounds found in cabinets, drawers, along countertops, and behind appliances.

🦒

Egg Cases (Oothecae)

Small, dark-brown, purse-shaped capsules found behind furniture, inside cabinets, and in crevices. Each German roach ootheca contains 30–40 eggs.

👃

Musty Odour

A strong, oily, musty smell that intensifies as the population grows. This odour comes from cockroach pheromones and can permeate food and surfaces.

👁

Smear Marks

Dark, irregular smear marks along walls, floors, and horizontal surfaces where cockroaches travel, especially in areas with water.

🥏

Shed Skins

Cockroaches moult multiple times as they mature. Translucent, cockroach-shaped skins accumulate near harbourage areas and behind appliances.

The Cockroach Problem in Ontario

Cockroaches are one of the most common and persistent pests in Ontario homes and businesses. The German cockroach (Blattella germanica) is by far the most frequent species encountered indoors, thriving in the warm, moist environments found in kitchens, bathrooms, and laundry rooms. Oriental cockroaches and American cockroaches are also present, particularly in basements, drains, and commercial properties.

What makes cockroaches so challenging is their reproductive speed. A single German cockroach female can produce up to 400 offspring in her lifetime. Because each egg case (ootheca) contains 30 to 40 eggs and development takes only about 60 days, a small infestation can explode into thousands within a few months. By the time you see cockroaches during the day, the population is typically well-established.

Serious Health Hazards

Cockroaches are not just unsightly — they are a documented health hazard. They carry bacteria including E. coli, Salmonella, and Staphylococcus on their bodies and legs, contaminating food, dishes, and kitchen surfaces as they forage. Their droppings, shed skins, and saliva contain potent allergens that are a leading trigger of asthma in children, particularly in urban multi-unit housing.

The World Health Organization has identified cockroaches as carriers of intestinal diseases including dysentery, diarrhoea, cholera, and typhoid fever. In Ontario's apartment buildings and food service establishments, cockroach infestations can also result in health code violations, fines, and reputational damage.

Why Gel Bait Outperforms Spraying

Over-the-counter cockroach sprays kill on contact but do not reach the 90% of the population hiding in wall voids, behind appliances, and inside electrical boxes. Worse, repellent sprays scatter cockroaches to new areas, spreading the infestation. PestoRest uses professional-grade gel bait formulations that cockroaches consume and carry back to harbourage areas. Through contact transfer and coprophagy (feeding on droppings), the active ingredient spreads through the population, including to nymphs and adults that never directly contacted the bait.

We combine gel bait with Insect Growth Regulators (IGRs) that prevent nymphs from reaching reproductive maturity, breaking the breeding cycle. For severe infestations, we add targeted crack-and-crevice treatments and dust applications in wall voids. This multi-pronged approach achieves population collapse rather than temporary knockdown.

Treating a single unit while neighbouring units remain infested leads to rapid re-infestation through shared plumbing and wall spaces. PestoRest works with property managers to inspect and treat multiple units simultaneously, ensuring lasting results across the building.

Cockroach Control FAQ — Ontario

Cockroaches are among the most resilient pests on Earth. German cockroaches reproduce at an extraordinary rate — a single female can produce 300 to 400 offspring in her lifetime. They are nocturnal and hide in tiny cracks during the day, making them hard to find. They have also developed resistance to many common pesticides, which is why professional gel bait and IGR treatments are far more effective than over-the-counter sprays.

Cockroaches are a significant health hazard. Their droppings, shed skins, and saliva contain allergens that trigger asthma attacks and allergic reactions, especially in children. They carry bacteria including E. coli and Salmonella on their bodies, contaminating food and kitchen surfaces. Cockroach allergens are one of the leading triggers of childhood asthma in urban environments across Ontario.

Cockroach treatment in Ontario typically costs between $250 and $1,000 depending on the species, severity of infestation, and size of the property. German cockroach infestations usually require multiple treatment visits for complete elimination. Multi-unit buildings may need coordinated treatment of adjacent units. PestoRest provides free inspections and transparent pricing before any work begins.

In multi-unit buildings, cockroaches travel between apartments through shared plumbing chases, electrical conduits, gaps around pipes, and ventilation systems. They can also be introduced through deliveries, grocery bags, and second-hand furniture or appliances. This is why treating a single unit is often insufficient — neighbouring units must be inspected and treated simultaneously for lasting results.
