Warning Signs

6 Signs You Have a Rodent Infestation

💩

Droppings

Small, dark pellets (3–6 mm for mice, up to 20 mm for rats) found along walls, inside cabinets, under sinks, and near food sources.

🔊

Scratching Noises

Sounds of scurrying, scratching, or gnawing in walls, ceilings, and attics — especially at night when rodents are most active.

🦷

Gnaw Marks

Fresh gnaw marks on food packaging, wood, drywall, plastic containers, and electrical wiring. Rats can gnaw through concrete and lead pipe.

👁

Grease Trails

Dark, oily rub marks along baseboards and walls where rodents repeatedly travel the same paths, leaving body oils and dirt behind.

📦

Nesting Material

Shredded paper, fabric, insulation, or plant matter gathered into hidden nests behind appliances, in wall voids, or inside stored boxes.

👃

Musky Odour

A stale, ammonia-like smell from rodent urine, especially strong in enclosed areas like closets, pantries, and attic spaces.

Why Rodents Are a Serious Threat in Ontario

Ontario's cold winters drive mice and rats indoors every fall. House mice (Mus musculus) and Norway rats (Rattus norvegicus) are the two most common rodent pests in the province, though deer mice are also found in rural and semi-rural areas. A single pair of mice can produce up to 60 offspring per year, meaning a small problem can escalate to a full infestation in a matter of weeks.

Rodents are not just a nuisance — they represent a genuine health and safety hazard. They contaminate food and cooking surfaces with urine, droppings, and hair. They carry over 35 diseases including hantavirus, leptospirosis, and salmonella. Their constant gnawing on electrical wiring is a documented cause of house fires, and their burrowing can undermine foundations and damage insulation.

Health Risks You Cannot Ignore

Hantavirus, transmitted through contact with deer mouse droppings and urine, can cause Hantavirus Pulmonary Syndrome (HPS), a potentially fatal respiratory illness. Salmonella and E. coli are spread when rodents walk across food preparation surfaces. Rodent allergens — proteins found in urine, droppings, and dander — are a significant trigger for asthma, particularly in children living in infested homes.

Even after rodents are removed, contaminated insulation and building materials may need to be cleaned or replaced to eliminate lingering health risks. PestoRest provides guidance on safe cleanup and can connect you with restoration specialists when needed.

Our Exclusion-First Approach

Trapping alone does not solve a rodent problem. If the entry points remain open, new rodents will simply take the place of those removed. That is why PestoRest leads with exclusion — a systematic process of identifying and sealing every gap, crack, and opening that rodents use to enter your home.

Our technicians inspect the entire exterior of your property, from the foundation to the roofline. We seal entry points with galvanized steel mesh, metal flashing, and commercial-grade sealants that rodents cannot gnaw through. Interior trapping is conducted simultaneously to remove rodents already inside. This combined approach delivers lasting results rather than temporary relief.

Property Damage Prevention

Beyond health concerns, rodents cause thousands of dollars in property damage each year across Ontario. They chew through wiring (creating fire hazards), destroy insulation (increasing heating costs), contaminate stored belongings, and damage structural wood. Early intervention and proper exclusion protect your home and your investment.
